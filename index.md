## About Me

<div class="prose-block">
Hello! I am <b>Cecile CHEN</b>, an undergraduate researcher in <a href="https://www.polyu.edu.hk/fh/" target="_blank">Language Sciences and Analytics at The Hong Kong Polytechnic University</a>. My academic interests sit at the intersection of psycholinguistics, neurolinguistics, cognitive science, speech-language sciences, and computational approaches to language. I am especially interested in how bilingual speakers perceive, adapt to, and produce speech, and how experimental and computational methods can help us understand language processing across tonal, orthographic, and developmental contexts. For more details, see my <a href="/assets/files/cv/main.pdf">CV</a>.
<br>
<br>
My current research experience spans EEG/ERP studies of tonal accent adaptation in Mandarin speech, child language and literacy intervention, lexical prosody in bilingual children with Childhood Apraxia of Speech, pragmatic sentiment analysis in social media text, and cross-dialectal intelligibility of Mandarin innovations. Across these projects, I combine behavioral experimentation, acoustic analysis, statistical modeling, NLP, and neuroimaging-oriented methods.
</div>

## Additional Notes

<div class="prose-block">
I am broadly drawn to questions about how linguistic structure becomes psychologically and neurally real: how listeners adapt to accents, how children learn prosody and orthography, how bilingual experience shapes perception and production, and how computational models can capture pragmatic meanings such as negation, irony, and emotion reversal. I hope to develop a research profile that bridges laboratory phonology, speech perception, neurolinguistics, language development, and data-driven language science.
</div>
