## Research Interests

<div class="prose-block">
My research interests center on the cognitive and neural mechanisms of speech and language processing. I am particularly interested in bilingual speech perception, tonal-language processing, accent adaptation, lexical prosody, child language intervention, Chinese orthographic learning, and computational approaches to pragmatics and sentiment. Methodologically, I am interested in combining experimental design, EEG/ERP, acoustic analysis, phonetic transcription, behavioral measures, statistical modeling, and NLP.
</div>

## Current Projects

1. Neurocognitive processing of tonal accent adaptation in Mandarin speech by Cantonese listeners: EEG/ERP evidence for predictive processing and perceptual adaptation in accented tonal speech.
2. Effector-independent VR-based and effector-specific tablet-based Chinese writing intervention among non-Chinese speaking students in Hong Kong.
3. Lexical prosodic perception and production changes in bilingual children with Childhood Apraxia of Speech (CAS), with attention to tonal-language intervention.
4. Reversal of emotions in social media: negation, opposition, irony, and pragmatic sentiment analysis for Chinese social media text.
5. Measuring unidirectional intelligibility of Taiwan Mandarin verb innovations from a Mainland Mandarin listener perspective.
{: .prose-block .project-list}

## Research Summary

<div class="prose-block">
A shared theme across my projects is the relation between language form and cognitive processing. In speech perception, I ask how bilingual listeners adapt to systematic variation such as tonal accent patterns. In child language and intervention research, I am interested in how prosodic, orthographic, and motor representations develop and how training design can support children with diverse linguistic backgrounds. In computational linguistics, I focus on how linguistic cues such as negation, opposites, and irony shift the emotional meaning of social media text.
</div>
